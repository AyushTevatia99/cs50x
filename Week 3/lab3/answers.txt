sort1 uses: Bubble Sort

How do you know?: Because its speed on the best case is faster than sort3 but slower than sort2, so its omega must be n and so its is Bubble sort.

sort2 uses: Merge Sort

How do you know?: Because it has the fastest speed on the best case. So it's omega must be n log n.

sort3 uses: Selection Sort

How do you know?: Because it took the longest time on the best case. So the omega must be n squared, and that is the omega of selection sort.
